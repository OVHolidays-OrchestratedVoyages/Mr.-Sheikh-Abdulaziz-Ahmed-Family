The Voyage

Eight days across three distinct movements. The hill country first — cool air, a working tea estate, and the particular quiet that comes with altitude. Then the south, where the landscape opens into dry forest, wild coastline, and wildlife. Then Colombo, briefly, before departure.

✦

Each stage has been arranged as a single continuous journey; transfers, experiences, and pace designed around Sheikh Abdulaziz Ahmed and his family. Nothing transitions without preparation. The island reveals itself differently at each altitude, and this voyage is built to hold all of them.

Day 01 · Arrival

Colombo: Helicopter to Hatton

Sheikh Abdulaziz Ahmed and family arrive in Colombo. The helicopter transfer begins at the private terminal; no waiting, no ordinary arrival sequence. The island unfolds from above: coastline first, then lowland, then the first green folds of the hill country rising ahead.

Transfer  ·  Colombo to Hatton  ·  Private Helicopter

Uga Halloowella receives the family on arrival. The estate sits within a working tea plantation above Hatton, check-in prepared in advance, the afternoon at the estate’s own pace. The highlands here have a particular quality of quiet that the rest of the voyage will not repeat.

02
Day 02 · The Hill Country

Tea, the Estate & the Naturalist’s Walk

The morning begins at 09:15 with the estate’s Tea Experience: a guided walk through the working plantation where pickers are sorting the morning’s harvest, a visit to the weighing station, and a 30-minute factory tour from fresh leaf to finished cup. The session closes with a tasting of freshly pressed Ceylon teas. Included in the stay.

The afternoon: the Habitat Hike departs at 15:00. Two and a half hours through the estate’s trails and tea fields, past the Hanuman Kovil and a viewpoint across Adam’s Peak. The route passes trap cameras placed by the naturalist in wildlife corridors. The evening presentation of their footage (palm civet, barking deer, leopard) is worth keeping free.

At 16:30, the estate serves its afternoon tea: Ceylon tea brewed to order alongside mini hoppers, kokis, and traditional highland sweets, on the patio or by the fire, depending on the afternoon’s weather. Included in the stay.

03
Day 03 · Hill Country Continued

Castlereagh at Dawn & the Tea Hills

An earlier start for those who wish it: kayaking on Castlereagh Reservoir departs at 06:00, while the mist is still on the water. The reservoir sits below the estate; the transfer is a short ride down. The morning at this hour has a particular stillness.

A quieter day is equally arranged. The estate’s cycling routes through the tea hills depart at 07:00 or 16:00, with mountain bikes and a tuk-tuk transfer to the starting point. Those who prefer to remain on the grounds may do so without arrangement.

The Pekoe Trail’s Stage 7 section, Kotagala to Norwood, is an easy walk through colonial-era tea estates, past the historic Chrysler’s Farm Tea Factory (est. 1870) and Singamalai Lake. For families, this is a full morning at a considered pace.

The afternoon tea service at 16:30 closes the day as it did the last.

Day 04 · Transfer to the Wild South

Air Transfer to Yala, Uga Chena Huts

Breakfast at the estate. The air transfer south to Yala follows; a different Sri Lanka from what the past three days have offered. The landscape shifts on descent: green and elevated gives way to flat, dry, and wide.

Three safaris are arranged across the stay. The family selects their preferred timing each day: morning departures at 06:00 or evening departures at 15:30.

Day 05 · First Safari

Yala: The Leopard Kingdom

The first safari enters Yala at first light. The lodge’s rangers identify the park’s leopards individually, each animal’s territory known, each morning’s drive navigated toward them with the patience that comes from years inside the park. Yala holds one of the highest leopard densities recorded anywhere in the world.

The park also holds elephant, sloth bear, spotted deer, buffalo, mugger crocodile, and more than 215 recorded bird species, including the rare black-necked stork.

For the children, the late morning at the lodge. The family’s programme for the day:

  • Sri Lankan Cooking Masterclass at 11:00: rice and two aromatic curries in clay pots, with the cultural significance of each recipe explained as the session unfolds
  • Curd-making immediately following: buffalo milk heated over an open hearth, set in a clay pot, served with kithul treacle
  • Both sessions designed for all ages, running together across two hours

Day 06 · Second Day in Yala

Bundala, the Beach & the Secret Sundowner

A second safari at the family’s preferred time. For those who wish to see the south through a different lens, the Bundala National Park safari departs at 05:00. A Ramsar-protected coastal wetland 45 minutes from the lodge, sheltering 197 documented bird species. During peak season, more than 100 can be sighted in a single session.

The coastal walk from the lodge departs at 16:00, a guided walk along the wild beach through dunes and rocky outcrops, past turtle nesting sites, and the overnight tracks of animals that pass through in the dark. The rangers lead; the coastline here is Yala’s least-visited edge.

At 16:30, the Secret Sundowner. The lodge guides the family to one of its curated wilderness viewpoints, chosen based on where wildlife has been moving. Drinks and cananés served as the light changes over the lagoon. Two hours at the edge of the park, before the dark settles over it.

Day 07 · The Road North

Final Safari, Galle Fort, Colombo

The third and final safari at the family’s preferred time. After check-out, the road north toward Colombo stops in Galle.

Transfer  ·  Yala to Galle to Colombo  ·  Private Road Transfer

Galle’s colonial fort is a 90-minute interlude: Portuguese ramparts from the 16th century, Dutch-era streets from the 17th, and the Indian Ocean on three sides. A different Sri Lanka from what the past six days have offered; older, quieter, and built from a different kind of history.

The journey continues to Colombo for the final night. Check-in to the city property before evening.

Day 08 · Departure

Colombo, Departure

The final day in Colombo is arranged around the family’s schedule and confirmed flight time. The city offers shopping, a relaxed coastal rhythm, and options suited to children and adults equally.

Colombo’s covered markets and boutique districts are an easy morning. The pace is the family’s own. Departure transfer to the international terminal at the confirmed time, the journey arranged to its last detail.
